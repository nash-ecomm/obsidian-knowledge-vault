## Brand Spotlight

> [!example] AG1 — Value Communication as Retention Engine
> AG1 ($600M+ business) discovered through UserTesting that customers were churning because they didn't understand the product's value — not because they disliked it. Their fix: all retention emails now revolve around **reminding subscribers why they signed up**. Tactics:
> - Study results shared at **30, 60, and 90-day** milestones to anchor the habit
> - Messaging segmented by health concern (gut health, immune function, nervous system) — the gut-health angle became their **most successful creative to date**
> - Economic framing: *"Whole body health for less than $3/day"* for price-sensitive segments
> - Proactive value emails before churn signals appear
>
> **What to steal:** Run a "why did you subscribe?" post-purchase survey, then build a segmented flow for each top answer. Don't wait for cancellation intent.
>
> Sources: [Tapcart/AG1 community breakdown](https://www.tapcart.com/blog/ag1-community-marketing-strategies) | [Panoramata AG1 profile](https://www.panoramata.co/marketing-strategy-brand/ag1)

> [!example] Olipop — Emoji-Led Deliverability + Urgency Signals
> Olipop's email program on Klaviyo has an average spam score of **-1** (highly deliverable). Notable patterns per [Panoramata's Olipop analysis](https://www.panoramata.co/marketing-strategy-brand/olipop) and [Benchmark DTC](https://www.benchmark-dtc.com/marketing/olipop):
> - **90.63% of emails** use emojis in subject lines (🥤 ✨ 👀 most common)
> - Standard first-order offer: **20% off** — consistent across acquisition flows
> - Seasonal timing + urgency copy drives meaningful open rate spikes
> - Health benefit framing (fiber, less sugar) paired with athlete partnerships builds trust signals
>
> **What to steal:** Test emoji in 90%+ of subject lines for CPG/consumable categories. The -1 spam score suggests clean list hygiene is non-negotiable — audit your suppression segments.

## Design Trends

**Dark mode is no longer optional.** [Litmus 2026 data](https://www.litmus.com/blog/trends-in-email-marketing) puts dark-mode email opens at **~35%** and climbing — heavily skewed toward 18–34 demos. Key execution notes from [Litmus's dark mode guide](https://www.litmus.com/blog/the-ultimate-guide-to-dark-mode-for-email-marketers):
- Use **off-white (not pure white) image backgrounds** on product shots — pure white creates harsh halos in dark mode (study how DoorDash handles food images)
- Avoid pure black + pure white combos — the contrast causes halation/discomfort
- Each major client (Gmail, Outlook, Apple Mail, Android) applies dark mode differently — test across all four

**Interactive elements are mainstream.** Per [Email Mavlers 2026 trends](https://www.emailmavlers.com/blog/email-design-trends-2026/):
- 97% of marketers used at least one interactive element in 2025 — expect near-100% in 2026
- Tactics worth testing: **live polls** (results update with every open), **scratch-off** reveals for discount announcements, preference center quizzes in welcome flows

**Minimalist + mobile-first.** [Litmus](https://www.litmus.com/blog/trends-in-email-marketing) clocks average email dwell time at **9 seconds**. One strong CTA beats a content-stuffed layout every time. Responsive design improves mobile click rates by **~15%** per Litmus benchmarks.

> [!info] Flows vs. Campaigns — The 18x Gap
> Email flows generate **41% of total email revenue** from just **5.3% of sends**. Flow RPR is **~18× higher** than campaigns. Nearly **48% of flow revenue** comes from new buyers (welcome, browse abandonment, cart abandonment). If your flows are underbuilt, that's your highest-leverage fix.

> [!warning] Open Rate Caveat
> Apple MPP inflates reported open rates by **10–15%** across Apple Mail users (64% MPP adoption). Use **click rate and RPR** as your real performance benchmarks — not opens.

**AI personalization impact:** Product recommendation blocks in emails lift average click rates to **3.75%** (vs. 2.44% baseline); top performers hit **8.79%**. If you're not using Klaviyo's Next Best Product blocks in post-purchase and browse-abandonment flows, you're leaving meaningful RPR on the table.

## Subscription Flows

### Skio — Smart Retries (Live)
Per [Skio's Payment Recovery docs](https://help.skio.com/docs/payment-recovery), Smart Retries now use subscriber **behavior patterns** (time of day, day of week, day of month) to optimize exactly when each failed-payment retry fires — not just a fixed daily interval. Discounts can be automatically layered onto specific retry phases to increase recovery likelihood. Default window: 14-day daily cycle, fully configurable.

### Recharge — Dunning + Enhanced Analytics
[Recharge's dunning guide](https://getrecharge.com/blog/prevent-passive-churn-with-automated-recharge-dunning-features/) covers its built-in passive churn prevention: auto-retries + customizable failed-charge emails + SMS alerts via RechargeSMS. **Enhanced Analytics** (Pro/Enterprise tier) gives per-cohort visibility into dunning recovery rates — useful for measuring whether your retry cadence is actually working.

### The Scale of the Problem
Involuntary churn (failed payments, not cancellations) accounts for **20–30% of all subscription churn** per [Churn Buster's 2026 dunning report](https://churnbuster.io/articles/best-dunning-management-software). Smart retry logic alone can improve recovery by **~36%**. Real-world benchmark: Doggy Do Good reduced churn from **~10% → ~1%** by combining exit surveys, segmented cancel offers, swap functionality, and personalized save messaging. Source: [Lucent Innovation subscription guide](https://www.lucentinnovation.com/resources/technology-posts/shopify-subscription-commerce-building-recurring-revenue-models-for-enterprise-dtc-brands-in-2026).

> [!info] Layering Churn Buster on Recharge
> For high-volume Recharge merchants: [Churn Buster](https://churnbuster.io) sits on top of Recharge's native dunning and adds email + SMS sequencing with smarter retry logic. Worth evaluating if your involuntary churn rate is above 5%.
