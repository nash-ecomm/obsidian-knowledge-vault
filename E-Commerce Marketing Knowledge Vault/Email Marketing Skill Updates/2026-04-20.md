## Brand Spotlight

> [!example] Olipop — Emoji-Led Subject Lines + Hard First-Order Offer
> Olipop uses emojis in **90.63% of emails**, leaning on 🥤 ✨ 👀 🍊 🍓 for sensory and seasonal signaling. Subject lines are consistently seasonal and time-anchored ("Spring flavors are here 🍊") to drive open intent through relevance, not discounting. The primary acquisition incentive remains a flat **20% off first order** — unusually direct for a brand with strong organic pull. ESP: Klaviyo. Source: [Panoramata — Olipop strategy](https://www.panoramata.co/marketing-strategy-brand/olipop) *(Tier 3 — treat as directional)*.
>
> **What to steal:**
> - Pick 3–5 brand-specific emojis and use them consistently — they become a visual signature in a crowded inbox
> - Pair seasonal subject lines with evergreen product relevance (not just holiday promotions)
> - If you're suppressing a first-order discount to protect margins, test the Olipop model: a single, prominent offer beats vague "value" messaging for cold subscribers

> [!example] Hims — Typography as Brand Identity in Email
> Hims & Hers emails are identifiable by typography-first design: bold, oversized serif and sans-serif headlines, minimal imagery, and tight visual hierarchy. The design system creates instant brand recognition even when inbox preview text is cut off. Per [MailCharts](https://www.mailcharts.com/companies/hims-email-marketing) and [Really Good Emails](https://reallygoodemails.com/search/emails/hims), their cadence runs high-frequency with consistent design language across promotional and transactional sends.
>
> **What to steal:**
> - Define 2–3 typographic rules for your emails (max type sizes, header style, weight hierarchy) and enforce them across all templates — consistency compounds into brand equity
> - Test a text-heavy, low-image send: some audiences respond better to editorial density than image-forward layouts

## Design Trends

Per [Litmus's 2026 Email Marketing Trends](https://www.litmus.com/blog/trends-in-email-marketing):

- **Interactive email is maturing**: polls, quizzes, product carousels, and color-swatch selectors are moving from experimental to expected for top DTC senders. Interactive elements show **2× conversion lift** vs. static content (Demand Metric via Litmus).
- **Dark mode support** is a growing brand risk: many brands' emails invert poorly in dark mode, damaging brand perception for a significant share of mobile users. Litmus's Dark Mode guide covers CSS targeting methods.
- **AMP for email remains a niche tool** with only **7% adoption** industry-wide — Apple Mail and Outlook don't support it, capping addressable reach at Gmail. Invest in CSS-based interactivity instead.

> [!warning] Don't Build for AMP
> The ROI math hasn't changed: AMP for email requires double the build effort for ~30% of your list at best. CSS-based carousels and hover states deliver 80% of the interactive experience with broad client compatibility and graceful fallback.

**Easiest implementation now:** Add a single inline poll to your post-purchase flow ("Which flavor would you try next?"). Zero-party data, no AMP dependency, and measurable engagement signal.

## Subscription Flows

### Stay.ai — Emerging Competitor to SKIO and Recharge
Per a [2026 platform comparison](https://zectox.is-a.dev/blog/shopify-subscription-apps-comparison-2026) *(Tier 3 — treat as directional)*, **Stay.ai** is emerging as the most aggressive competitor in the DTC subscription stack with AI-native differentiation:

- **Theme Engine** for custom subscriber portal UX
- Pricing: **$499/month + 1% transaction fee** — slightly below SKIO for high-volume brands

> [!warning] Evaluate Before Migrating
> Platform migrations for subscriptions carry real churn risk during the transition window. Treat Stay.ai as a roadmap signal — if SKIO or Recharge doesn't ship AI-native churn scoring in H1 2026, it becomes a meaningful evaluation trigger.

### Dunning Best Practice — Decouple Emails from Retries
Per [Recharge's dunning documentation](https://getrecharge.com/blog/prevent-passive-churn-with-automated-recharge-dunning-features/) and [ChurnBuster best practices](https://churnbuster.io/dunning-best-practices): the highest-performing dunning stacks **front-load payment retries before notifying the customer**. If the retry succeeds silently, the subscriber never knows there was a failure — preserving the relationship and skipping unnecessary friction.

Recommended sequence:
1. Retry payment silently on Day 1, Day 3 (no email)
2. Customer notification email only if Day 3 retry fails
3. Continue retries + emails on Days 5, 7, 10, 14
4. Cancel at Day 14 with a reactivation offer triggered by the cancellation event in Klaviyo

**Decline type matters:** Retry insufficient funds around payday windows (1st/15th); route expired cards immediately to a card-update email with a direct update link — retries won't recover expired cards.
