## Brand Spotlight

> [!example] Liquid Death — Collab-to-Email Funnel
> Liquid Death's [e.l.f. Cosmetics collab](https://campaignlive.com/article/beauty-lovers-will-die-liquid-death-elf-cosmetics-newest-drop/1944757) (reported by Campaign US) is their latest in a string of brand collabs (previously: Van Leeuwen ice cream). The tactical play isn't just PR — limited-edition collab drops create a **built-in email urgency mechanic**: announce → waitlist → launch → sold-out. Each phase is a natural email trigger.
>
> Their social strategy ([Marketing Brew, Jan 2026](https://www.marketingbrew.com/stories/2026/01/12/liquid-death-social-marketing-strategy)) is deliberately low-volume: *"We're not doing volume play"* — a handful of breakthrough pieces per month rather than daily content. The same logic applies to email: **fewer sends, higher creative bar, stronger urgency when something does land**.
>
> **What to steal:**
> - Map a collab or limited-edition drop to a 3-email sequence: teaser → early access (subscribers only) → last chance
> - Collab partners should be genuinely unexpected — the e.l.f. × water brand cognitive dissonance is exactly what makes the subject line worth opening
> - Gate early access to your email list to convert social followers into subscribers

> [!example] Olipop — Amazon PR Box → Influencer Machine
> Olipop is [turning fans into influencers for pennies using Amazon PR boxes](https://www.marketingdive.com/news/olipop-turns-fans-into-influencers-for-pennies-with-pr-boxes-on-amazon/751392/) (Marketing Dive). The mechanism: send product to micro-fans, who post organic UGC, which Olipop then puts paid media behind — "unpaid, unprompted, unbriefed." Cost per creator is a fraction of traditional influencer spend.
>
> The email angle: Olipop's [[Klaviyo]] program segments by flavor preference and health benefit (gut health, fiber, less sugar). Post-purchase flows reinforce the health story, not just the product. Their spam score of **-1** (highly deliverable) is a direct result of strong list hygiene and behavioral segmentation — they don't batch-and-blast.
>
> **What to steal:**
> - Build a "superfan" segment (3+ purchases, high open/click engagement) and offer them early access or PR boxes — it costs almost nothing and generates real UGC
> - Reinforce your product's health/lifestyle benefit in post-purchase flows, not just the next offer; Olipop's gut-health messaging in email extends the purchase rationale

## Design Trends

**The "Oasis of Calm" pattern.** Per [Designmodo's 2026 Email Design Trends](https://designmodo.com/email-design-trends/) and [Email Mavlers](https://www.emailmavlers.com/blog/email-design-trends-2026/), the breakout design template of 2026 is the deliberate strip-down: one full-bleed product image, 1–2 lines of copy, one CTA. No secondary offers, no promotional banners, no social links in the body.

The Polestar electric vehicle brand is the cited benchmark — their emails use almost no elements beyond a hero visual and a single action. The data rationale: average email dwell time is **9 seconds** (Litmus). Cluttered layouts punish brands whose audience makes split-second decisions.

> [!info] The Minimalist Stack in Practice
> - **Hero image**: product-only or lifestyle with no text overlay (text in image = rendering failure risk)
> - **Body copy**: 2–3 lines max, lead with the benefit not the feature
> - **CTA**: one button, above the fold on mobile, verb-first label ("Shop Spring Drop" not "Learn More")
> - **Footer**: required legal only — no social grid, no secondary navigation

**Mobile parity is here.** [Email Mavlers](https://www.emailmavlers.com/blog/email-design-trends-2026/) notes 2026 is the inflection point where mobile engagement equals desktop. The implication isn't just responsive layouts — it means designing at 375px width first, then expanding to desktop. Any element that doesn't look right at 375px gets cut.

## Benchmarks & Data

Key update from [[2026-03-30]] benchmarks: flows vs. campaigns gap has widened further. Sourced from [Klaviyo 2026 Benchmarks](https://www.klaviyo.com/products/email-marketing/benchmarks) and [MHI Growth Engine DTC Benchmarks](https://mhigrowthengine.com/blog/dtc-email-benchmarks-2026/):

| Metric | Email Campaigns | Email Flows | Flows Advantage |
|--------|----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Click Rate | 1.69% | 5.58% | **3.3× higher** |
| Placed Order Rate | Baseline | 13× baseline | **13× higher** |
| % of Total Revenue | ~59% | ~41% | — |
| % of Total Sends | ~94.7% | ~5.3% | Flows punch far above weight |

> [!warning] The Send Efficiency Gap
> Flows account for only **5.3% of all sends** but drive **41% of revenue**. Every hour spent building a new campaign is competing with the ROI of improving an underperforming flow. Audit your abandon cart, post-purchase, and winback flows before planning next month's campaign calendar.

**Email-attributed revenue ceiling:** Well-optimized DTC programs generate **20–40% of total business revenue** via email. Below 20%? Flows are underbuilt. Source: [MHI Growth Engine](https://mhigrowthengine.com/blog/dtc-email-benchmarks-2026/).

## Subscription Flows

### Timing-Based Retry Logic — The Underused Lever
Last week's note covered that smart retry logic improves payment recovery by **~36%** ([Churn Buster](https://churnbuster.io/articles/best-dunning-management-software)). The next-level detail: **decline type determines retry timing**, not just retry count.

Per [Churn Buster's Shopify Dunning Guide](https://churnbuster.io/articles/shopify-dunning):

| Decline Type | Recommended Retry Window | Rationale |
|--------------|--------------------------|-----------|
| Insufficient funds | Early morning, post-payday (1st/15th) | Funds available after paycheck hits |
| Expired card | Prompt email → immediate retry after update | Time-sensitive; no point delaying |
| Do-not-honor | 48–72hr wait, then retry once | Issuer-level block; retrying same-day gets re-blocked |
| Generic decline | Standard 3-day interval, 3 attempts | Widest net for ambiguous failures |

> [!info] Platform Implementation
> - **Recharge**: Native retry cadence is configurable per interval; failed-charge email templates are customizable under Notification Settings — add RechargeSMS alerts for mobile-first subscribers ([Recharge dunning guide](https://getrecharge.com/blog/prevent-passive-churn-with-automated-recharge-dunning-features/))
> - **Skio**: Smart Retries (live) use behavioral patterns to pick optimal retry timing per subscriber — toggle in Payment Recovery settings
> - **Churn Buster**: For high-volume merchants on Recharge who want decline-type-specific logic, layering Churn Buster adds the granular routing Recharge's native tools don't provide
