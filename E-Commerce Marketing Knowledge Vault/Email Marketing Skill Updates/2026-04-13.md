# DTC Email Marketing Weekly — April 13, 2026

## Klaviyo Updates

### RCS Campaigns — Native Product Carousels
Per the [Klaviyo Q1 2026 Community Update](https://community.klaviyo.com/product-updates-announcements-51/klaviyo-q1-2026-product-updates-composer-launch-key-feature-releases-19146) and [What's New page](https://www.klaviyo.com/whats-new), brands can now send **RCS campaigns featuring product carousels** with images, pricing, and "Shop Now" buttons. Customers browse and initiate checkout directly from the message — no site visit required for the initial intent step.

> [!info] RCS Coverage Reality Check
> RCS requires Android + RCS-enabled carrier. Apple added RCS in iOS 18 but without rich business messaging features yet. Practically: **estimate 25–35% of your SMS list is addressable with RCS** in the US today. Not an email replacement — a high-intent supplement for winback and abandon-cart.

### Next Best Product (NBP) Expands to SMS, RCS, WhatsApp
NBP recommendations — previously email-only — now fire across SMS, RCS, and WhatsApp, dynamically surfacing products based on each subscriber's purchase history. No additional setup required if NBP is already configured in your email flows. Source: [Klaviyo What's New](https://www.klaviyo.com/whats-new).

### Customer Hub — WooCommerce + On-Site Segment Banners
Two expansions from the [Q1 product update](https://community.klaviyo.com/product-updates-announcements-51/klaviyo-q1-2026-product-updates-composer-launch-key-feature-releases-19146):
1. **WooCommerce support**: Customer Hub is no longer Shopify-only — WooCommerce merchants can now deploy Klaviyo's on-site loyalty and service experience natively.
2. **Segment-targeted banners**: Dynamic banners at the top of your storefront, visible to up to 100% of visitors, targeted by segment — no custom code. Deliver personalized offers, VIP recognition, or announcements without a dev ticket.

### SMS Compliance — April 11 Effective Date
Enhanced SMS opt-out coverage went live **April 11** with expanded keyword and intent-based unsubscribe handling. Verify your opt-out keyword configurations in Klaviyo's SMS settings and test that unsubscribes process correctly.

## Brand Spotlight

> [!example] Glossier — Community Membership as Email Acquisition Engine
> Glossier's community-driven model converts members at a reported **96% higher LTV and 3× purchase frequency** vs. non-members. The email play: community membership is the access credential. Subscribers receive early product drops, exclusive colorways, and founder-letter updates that non-subscribers don't. Email isn't a retention channel — it's the delivery mechanism for belonging.
>
> ⚠️ *LTV/frequency figures sourced from [tyb.xyz DTC Engagement Guide 2026](https://www.tyb.xyz/blog/customer-engagement-strategy-for-dtc-brands---the-complete-2026-guide-to-building-engagement-that-compounds) (Tier 3 — treat as directional). The structural model is independently documented.*
>
> **What to steal:**
> - Create a named "insider" tier in your list — give it an identity, not just a tag. Make it feel like membership.
> - Route insider-tier subscribers to a separate welcome flow that leads with access and exclusivity, not a discount code
> - Replace "10% off when you subscribe" with early-access drop invitations as your primary opt-in offer

## Design Trends

Interactive email is moving from experiment to expectation. Per [Litmus's 2026 Email Marketing Trends report](https://www.litmus.com/blog/trends-in-email-marketing):

- **Interactive elements generate 2× conversions** vs. static content (Demand Metric, via Litmus)
- **50% of recipients** want to interact more with content inside their emails (Dyspatch, via Litmus)
- Top patterns gaining traction: inline polls, product color-swatch selectors, image carousels, add-to-cart buttons

> [!warning] AMP for Email — Still Not Worth It for Most Brands
> Five years after launch, **only 7% of email marketers** use AMP for email ([Litmus](https://www.litmus.com/blog/email-trends-amp-for-email)). Apple Mail and Outlook don't support it, capping addressability at Gmail users. Invest instead in CSS-based interactivity (hover states, accordions, carousels) that degrades gracefully across all clients.

**Lowest-friction entry point:** Add an inline NPS or flavor/preference poll to your post-purchase flow. Zero-party data gain, no AMP dependency, and it registers as interactive in Litmus's heat maps.

The industry shift toward **revenue per subscriber per month** as the primary health metric is now mainstream among advanced DTC operators. Sources: [Klaviyo 2026 Benchmarks](https://www.klaviyo.com/products/email-marketing/benchmarks), [MHI Growth Engine](https://mhigrowthengine.com/blog/dtc-email-benchmarks-2026/).

| Click Rate | 2–5% | 2.44% | Most reliable engagement signal |
| Unsubscribe Rate | — | 0.89% | — |
| Bounce Rate | — | 2.48% | — |
| Email-Attributed Revenue | **20–40%** of total | — | Fully optimized programs only |

> [!warning] Open Rate Reality
> DTC open rates of 30–45% are inflated **20–40 percentage points** by Apple MPP pre-loading tracking pixels. Use click rate and revenue per email as your primary signals. Open rate is valid only for relative comparisons within your own list over time — not cross-brand benchmarking.

## Subscription Flows

### Skio Cancel Flow — Drag-and-Drop Builder + Analytics Dashboard
Per [Skio's updated Cancel Flow docs](https://help.skio.com/docs/cancel-flow-best-practices-guide), the multi-step cancel flow now features a **drag-and-drop interface** for building retention sequences. The [Cancel Flow Dashboard](https://help.skio.com/docs/cancel-flow-dashboard) now surfaces:

- **True save rate** (saves ÷ total cancel attempts)
- Save action conversion by **cancel reason** (price, too much product, pausing, etc.)
- Comparison of cancel-flow churn vs. passive churn vs. merchant cancellations

| Cancel Flow Treatment | Best Fit Cancel Reason |
|----------------------|------------------------|
| Skip Next Order | "Too much product" |
| Pause Subscription | "Traveling" / "taking a break" |
| Discount Offer | Price sensitivity |
| Product Swap | Wrong variant / flavor |
| Frequency Change | High-volume replenishment |

> [!info] Skio Dunning Default
> Skio's default payment recovery runs a **14-day daily retry cycle**. Layer decline-type-specific timing on top (see [[2026-04-06]] for retry logic by decline type) for maximum recovery without subscriber fatigue.
