## Deep Dive: Shopify Markets Pro — International Expansion at New Economics

Shopify's international commerce stack got meaningfully cheaper in Q4 2025. According to [AdNabu's Markets Pro overview](https://blog.adnabu.com/shopify/shopify-markets/), the October 14 pricing change cut the transaction fee from **6.5% to 3.5%** and currency conversion from **2.5% to 1.5%** for newly onboarded merchants — dropping the combined cost on a $100 international order from $9.00 to $5.00. New merchants also receive **daily payouts** via Shopify Payments rather than weekly settlement, improving cash flow for brands processing high cross-border volume. The prior fee structure made international expansion economically marginal below $2M ARR; the updated structure pushes that crossover point down to roughly $500K.

What Markets Pro actually handles matters here: it's not just currency display. The platform manages **130+ currencies** with automatic rate conversion at checkout, calculates and collects **duties and import taxes** before payment (eliminating carrier-billed surprises on delivery), surfaces local payment methods by market, and handles cross-border VAT compliance in one admin. The 2025 [Markets API](https://www.shopify.com/blog/international-import-shipping) gives operators programmatic control over region-specific behavior — different product availability, custom pricing by market, inventory routing — without maintaining separate storefronts per country. Brands running parallel Shopify stores for US, EU, and AUS can consolidate into a single admin with market logic handled via API, eliminating the app-stack duplication and SEO fragmentation that come with multi-store architectures.

The tariff environment makes duty collection at checkout urgent, not optional. [Shopify's April 2026 international shipping guide](https://www.shopify.com/blog/international-import-shipping) documents the two structural changes operators need to act on: the U.S. **de minimis exemption was eliminated August 29, 2025** — duties now apply to all U.S.-bound imports regardless of value, ending the era of duty-free sub-$800 packages. A further **10% temporary import duty** under Trade Act Section 122 activated February 24, 2026 for 150 days. For DTC brands manufacturing outside the U.S. and shipping to American customers, these policy changes require either repricing to absorb landed costs, or activating Shopify's built-in duty calculation so customers see true costs at checkout before they buy — not as a billed surprise at delivery.

> [!tip] Takeaway
> Run the fee comparison before the next planning cycle: tally your current international processing cost (payment processor + manual currency conversion + separate storefront ops overhead) against the new Markets Pro rate. At 3.5% + 1.5%, the break-even against a DIY setup is much lower than in 2024. If you're already on Shopify, the migration path is within your existing admin.

> [!warning] De Minimis Is Gone — Enable Duty Collection Now
> If you ship non-U.S.-manufactured goods to American customers and haven't enabled duty collection at checkout, you're either absorbing costs silently or creating post-purchase billing surprises that spike returns. Shopify admin → Settings → Taxes and duties → enable landed cost display. Free to configure; 0.5% fee per order where duties are calculated (temporarily reduced rate since February 2025).

## Deep Dive: Shopify Audiences — The Free Paid Media Multiplier Most Plus Merchants Ignore

[Shopify Audiences](https://www.shopify.com/audiences) is Shopify's first-party data network for paid media — a free tool for Plus merchants using Shopify Payments (U.S. and Canada only) that builds high-intent custom audiences for Meta and Google by tapping Shopify's anonymized cross-merchant purchase graph. The mechanism: Shopify observes purchase behavior across hundreds of millions of buyers on all Shopify stores. Audiences uses that network to identify buyers who are statistically likely to purchase in your specific product category — not just people who've visited your site — and exports those audiences directly to your ad platform.

The performance uplift is driven by a technical improvement most brands underestimate. As documented in [PageFly's Audiences implementation guide](https://pagefly.io/blogs/shopify/shopify-audiences), connecting Audiences pushes Meta CAPI event match quality from **50–65%** (browser pixel only) to **85–95%** server-side. What that means practically: Meta's algorithm has been making targeting and optimization decisions on noisy, degraded signal for most stores since iOS 14. Higher match quality means fewer wasted impressions, faster audience learning, and better lookalike seed data — which compounds into a documented **30% ROAS increase** and **40%+ CAC reduction** in merchant implementations. On Google, Audiences syncs to Customer Match, enabling LTV-weighted lookalikes that outperform broad interest targeting for category-specific DTC products.

Shopify has published three brand case studies demonstrating the range of outcomes: **Laura Geller Beauty** documented ROAS and revenue improvements post-activation; **Mac Duggal** more than doubled its retargeting audience size; **Nathan James** (furniture DTC) identified 500+ net-new buyers it couldn't have reached through in-platform lookalikes alone. The tool is **free for all Shopify Plus merchants using Shopify Payments** — setup under an hour, no ongoing cost, no third-party integration required.

The access constraints are real and worth noting. Audiences requires: Shopify Plus plan, Shopify Payments as the active processor, and a U.S. or Canada store. Brands on lower-tier plans, using Stripe/Braintree/other processors, or operating outside North America don't currently qualify. For those who do qualify, this is likely the highest-ROI configuration task available this quarter.

> [!tip] Takeaway
> If you're Shopify Plus + Shopify Payments (US/CA) and haven't activated Audiences: go to Shopify admin → Marketing → Audiences, connect your Meta Ads account, and let it run for 30 days to build audience lists. Then A/B test one campaign using an Audiences-generated list against your current best Meta lookalike — 14-day window, equal budget, document ROAS delta. The benchmark says 20–30% improvement is the base expectation.

> [!info] Shopify Audiences Eligibility at a Glance
> | Requirement | Notes |
> |---|---|
> | Shopify Plus | Required — not available on lower tiers |
> | Shopify Payments active | Third-party processors (Stripe, etc.) not eligible |
> | U.S. or Canada store | International expansion of eligibility not yet announced |
> | Meta or Google Ads account | Connect one or both |
> | Monthly cost | **$0** |
