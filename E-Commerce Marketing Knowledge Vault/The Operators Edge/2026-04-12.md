## Deep Dive: Shopify Managed Markets — Cross-Border DTC in a Tariff-Volatile World

Cross-border selling has always had one dominant friction point: buyers abandon international checkouts when they anticipate — or receive — surprise customs fees on delivery. [Shopify Managed Markets](https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/international/managed-markets/overview) (the rebrand of Markets Pro, powered by a Global-e partnership) eliminates this entirely by collecting duties and import taxes **at checkout** (Delivered Duty Paid, DDP) and remitting them to local tax authorities on the merchant's behalf. The result: no surprise charges on delivery, no customs holds, no international return loops. For the buyer, the experience is indistinguishable from a domestic purchase.

The commercial impact is documented and significant. Caden Lane, a baby apparel DTC brand, deployed Managed Markets and [more than doubled the countries they sell to](https://changelog.shopify.com/posts/smarter-international-pricing-with-managed-markets), resulting in a **692% lift in international sales**. The merchant economics are transparent: a **1.5% FX conversion fee** applies when prices auto-convert to local currencies, plus a **0.5% duties/tax calculation fee** (temporarily lowered as of February 2025). Global-e handles [local VAT registration, remittance, and local payment method acceptance](https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/international/duties-and-import-taxes) in 150+ countries — no foreign entity setup, no local tax counsel required.

The tariff environment makes DDP operationally critical in 2026. The US Supreme Court invalidated IEEPA-based tariffs on February 20, 2026; Customs stopped collecting them on February 24. A **new 10% import duty under Section 122** replaced them the same day, applying for 150 days. USMCA-compliant imports from Canada and Mexico remain exempt, as do certain electronics, pharmaceuticals, and agricultural products. For DTC brands shipping internationally or sourcing goods with tariff exposure, Managed Markets' DDP model locks in landed cost at checkout — eliminating the post-shipment duty reconciliation that has caught operators flat-footed during every tariff cycle. US and Canadian merchants can now book **DDP shipping labels via DHL Express and DHL eCommerce** directly in Shopify admin.

The newest capability to activate: **adaptive pricing**, rolled out to all existing Managed Markets merchants on March 26, 2026. Previously, auto-converting a $49 USD product to euros might display €45.73 — a price that signals "computer-generated" to price-aware international buyers. [Adaptive pricing](https://changelog.shopify.com/posts/smarter-international-pricing-with-managed-markets) automatically rounds local prices to psychologically clean thresholds (€49, €45, €50) without requiring market-by-market manual overrides in Shopify Markets.

> [!tip] Takeaway
> If you sell internationally and Managed Markets isn't active, your #1 conversion gap is almost certainly surprise duties at delivery. Enable in Shopify admin → Markets → Manage. Confirm DDP labels are configured via DHL Express for your top 3 export markets. Activate adaptive pricing immediately — it's included at no additional cost and eliminates the awkward converted-price problem overnight.

## Deep Dive: Shopify POS Pro — The DTC-to-Retail Expansion Playbook

The pure-play online DTC model of 2020–2023 is being revised. Paid media CAC has compressed return timelines, and the DTC brands growing fastest in 2026 are adding physical retail as an acquisition and LTV channel — not a backup plan. [Shopify POS Pro](https://www.shopify.com/enterprise/blog/unified-commerce-retail) is the infrastructure that makes retail expansion operationally tractable for a lean DTC team without a dedicated retail tech stack.

The core of POS Pro's value is a **truly unified commerce backend**. POS Pro runs on the same Shopify admin as your online store — one product catalog, one inventory pool, one customer database. When a store associate sells the last unit of a colorway in-person, that variant goes out of stock online simultaneously. When a customer makes their first in-store purchase, they enter Klaviyo with location and product data intact. BOPIS (buy online, pick up in-store) and ship-from-store are native, not middleware-dependent. An EY Future Proofing Retail study quantified the payoff: merchants on [Shopify POS see **8.9% average annual sales uplift** from omnichannel features](https://www.shopify.com/enterprise/blog/faherty-unified-commerce) and run **22% lower total cost of ownership** vs. rival setups (middleware eliminated, duplicate workflows removed). Implementation runs **20% faster** than competitors.

The brand evidence is consistent across scale. Faherty completed POS deployment across [**80+ retail locations in three months**](https://www.shopify.com/enterprise/blog/faherty-unified-commerce) — including remote store setup that eliminated on-site IT support requirements. Nutrition Warehouse unified **120+ stores** on a single Shopify POS instance in six months. Tony's Chocolonely migrated from a custom platform to Shopify unified commerce and hit **70% US revenue growth** in the following period. Serge Blanco (50+ European menswear locations) cut platform costs **75–80%** versus their previous Magento stack and hit **5% in-store conversion rates**.

POS Pro costs **$89/month per location** (included in certain Shopify Plus plans). The incremental capabilities over POS Lite: advanced inventory management (demand forecasting, purchase orders, inter-location transfers), staff roles and permissions, custom location-level reporting, configurable smart grid layouts (a Winter 2026 update per [Echidna's POS Hub breakdown](https://echidna.co/blog/shopify-pos-hub-winter-2026)), and full BOPIS/ship-from-store workflows. The break-even is simple: if BOPIS or ship-from-store drives 5–10 incremental monthly orders at a typical DTC AOV of $75–$120, the $89/month fee is covered in margin on those orders alone.

> [!example] DTC-to-Retail POS Launch Checklist
> 1. **Catalog audit** — confirm all SKUs, variants, and bundles are clean in Shopify admin before POS goes live; POS pulls directly from the online catalog
> 2. **Klaviyo tagging** — build a Shopify Flow to tag `first-purchase-in-store` on in-store first-time buyers and route them into a separate post-purchase sequence
> 3. **BOPIS activation** — Shopify admin → Locations → [store] → Allow pickup; test the full buyer flow end-to-end before launch
> 4. **Staff permissions** — lock discount override authority to manager-level POS roles before handing devices to store staff

> [!warning] POS Lite vs. POS Pro: Know the Feature Gates
> POS Lite (free on all Shopify plans) covers basic in-person selling. POS Pro gates the features that make multi-location retail efficient: smart inventory management, inter-location transfers, advanced reporting, and complete BOPIS workflows. If you're opening more than one location or running any buy-online-pick-up-in-store scenario, Lite will create operational gaps quickly. Budget POS Pro ($89/location/month) from day one of any retail expansion plan.
