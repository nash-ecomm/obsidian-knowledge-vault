# Black Friday Preparations

We are only 56 days away from the biggest sales event of the year. But I can't wait to share with you the blueprint that has brought Nordstorm $100,000,000 in additional annual sales and nearly $30,000,000 in profits. The promotion was so successful that it's still going strong 20 years later. And it cost almost nothing...

## What did Nordstorm do?

Nordstorm customers had to spend $700 to be invited to their Anniversary Sale. The result was that customers spent 18% more before the sale to offset FOMO. The hack was smart segmentation of customers who just needed that little extra purchase to qualify for VIP sale access. And that sale was going to happen anyway: Nordstorm leveraged a sale to sell more full price. We will replicate this process for your brand for the craziest sale of the year: Black Friday.

## How to create a Black Friday Early Access Campaign

## Identify the right threshold

The first piece of the puzzle is to identify your individual threshold (that $700 barrier from Nordstorm) at which people qualify for access. The goal is to:

- Incentivize enough customers to make a full-price purchase
- Balance to make it achievable while maximizing profit
- Make access something to strive for, not something everyone gets anyway.

Therefore, it makes sense to target 2x buyers and 1x buyers with high spending to make an additional purchase. It's the ideal combination of having a large enough audience while keeping access exclusive. So in order to find a good target for an entry barrier, we should have a look at the average net revenue (after product returns) of 3-time buyers. First, segment your 3x buyers. Make sure that they have exactly 3 purchases, excluding swaps (product exchanges).

The next step is to look at the average net revenue of these customers. Customers with 3 orders contributed in average for $335 net revenue. So $350 net revenue could be a great threshold to qualify for our "Black Friday Early Access".

Now comes a little twist... The threshold should be set so that one additional purchase is enough to qualify for early access - but it should be a regular size order. Not just a small add-on.

## Segment the cash cows

The next step is segmenting customers who only need one more purchase to qualify for the "Black Friday Early Access". Target this segment with the following communication strategy:

- Start 7 days before the early access date
- Send a total of 3-5 emails in that 7 days
- Run early access for a total of 2-3 days
- Create FOMO
- Communicate the missing revenue to reach the status, ideally with a progress bar.

## Setup the access

Now it's time to set up the collection and grant access rights. Identify the customers who will get access:

Customers with net revenue ≥ $500. These customers will be notified 3 days prior to the start of "Black Friday Early Access", explaining why they qualify and the benefits of early access to the sale, such as:

- Shop before anybody else
- Best deals on high demand items
- Additional discounts
- Faster shipping

And it cost almost nothing...
